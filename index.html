Introduction to Heart

Heart is a hollow, muscular organ that pumps blood throughtout the body. The heart depends on its electrical system to deliver signals and make the heartbeat normally. Such heart whhich contracts from an electrical impulse within the muscle is termed as myogenic heart. Read more

Structure of heart

The heart has four chambers.
i. Right Atrium: Receives deoxygenated blood from the body.
ii. Right Ventricle: Pumps deoxygenated blood to the lungs.
iii. Left Atrium: Receives oxygenated blood from the lungs.
iv. Left Ventricle: Pumps oxygenated blood to the rest of the body.

Function of heart

  • Pumping oxygenated blood to the body.
  • Pumping deoxygenated blood to the lungs for oxygenation.
  • Maintaining blood pressure.
  • Transporting nutrients, hormones, and waste products.

Cardiac Cycle

The cardiac cycle consists of two main phases: systole and diastole.
Systole: The phase when the heart muscles contract, pumping blood out of the chambers.
Diastole: The phase when the heart muscles relax, allowing the chambers to fill with blood.
